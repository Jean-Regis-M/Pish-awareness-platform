Security Awareness Tips

Check Email Headers

Always verify the sender's email address, not just the display name. Look for subtle misspellings or unusual domains.

Hover Before Clicking

Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking. Be suspicious of URLs that don't match the claimed destination.

Beware of Urgency

Phishing emails often create a false sense of urgency. Legitimate organizations rarely demand immediate action.

Verify Through Official Channels

If unsure, contact the organization directly using official contact information, not the details in the suspicious email.

Phishing Identification Hints

Check the sender's email address

The domain "company-portal.net" is not our company's official domain.

Urgency and threats

Legitimate HR communications don't threaten permanent loss of benefits with a 2-hour deadline.

Suspicious link

Hover over the button to see it doesn't point to our company's website.

Vague reference

The "inconsistency in your last submitted vacation accrual report" is intentionally vague to create concern.
